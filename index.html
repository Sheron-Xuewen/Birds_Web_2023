Art and Nature's Rhythm

The seal's position is designed with reference to the Solar Terms Chart. The round eye of birds metaphorizes the sun, while seals in various positions metaphorize the position of the earth relative to the sun at this solar term. The design symbolized the flow of different solar terms and shows the cycle of time and the change of seasons.

The single eye of the bird is the core of the picture. Its gaze was firmly fixed on the surrounding red seals just like sunlight shining on the earth. The bird's gaze not only guides the viewer's gaze, but also hints at life's desire and sensitivity to light and seasonal changes, as well as the profound connection between life and nature. I hope that this will subtly integrate the periodicity of solar terms and the harmony of celestial bodies, thereby triggering the audience to think deeply about the laws of nature and the rhythm of life, and reminding people to pay attention to and appreciate the exquisite harmony of nature.

Inspired by Bada Shanren

My artwork has been influenced by Bada Shanren. I used a single ink color and rough and strong lines to outline the form of birds, capturing their vividness and dynamics. Appropriate white space not only enhances the visual artistic effect of the artwork, but also provides the audience with a space for imagination and thinking.

Different from the creative mood of Bada Shanren, I convey more of a sense of freedom that is detached from the world and a proud and free spirit that is not afraid of the changes of the seasons. My spirit and pursuit are placed in it, praising the birds that still live tenaciously and beautifully in the changing climate.

The Beginning of Spring

Lunar Phase: The Beginning of Spring
Lunar Date: Around the 1st to 3rd day of the 1st lunar month
Calendar Date: Approximately February 4th or 5th
Bird Species: Magpie
Feature: The magpie's tail is long and stiff.
Design Concept: The magpie is known as ' happiness magpie' in Chinese, symbolizing fortune. I have selected it as the emblem for the first solar term, representing the magpie as a harbinger of spring's arrival.

The Spring Equinox

Lunar Phase: The Spring Equinox
Lunar Date: Around the 15th to 18th day of the 2nd lunar month
Calendar Date: Approximately March 20th or 21st
Bird Species: Swallow (carrying a fruit)
Feature: The swallow's tail is scissor-shaped, and it often carries things in its beak.
Design Concept: In Chinese culture, swallows are beloved for nesting under eaves and living close to humans. These migratory birds, flying south annually and returning each spring, symbolize spring and yearning for home and family.

Grain Buds

Lunar Phase: Grain Buds
Lunar Date: Around the 15th to 17th day of the 4th lunar month
Calendar Date: Approximately May 21st or 22nd
Bird Species: Crane
Feature: The crane stands with long legs, a short tail, and wings folded elegantly.
Design Concept: Grain Buds marks the season of grain growth and is also the period of migration and breeding for cranes. Both of these elements symbolize prosperity and abundance in traditional Chinese culture.

The Summer Solstice

Lunar Phase: The Summer Solstice
Lunar Date: Around the 10th to 12th day of the 5th lunar month
Calendar Date: Approximately June 21st or 22nd
Bird Species: Sparrow
Feature: The sparrow has a light body, a round head, a small beak, and short legs.
Design Concept: Summer Solstice is a busy farming season in China, and sparrows are known to feed on insects in the fields, indirectly promoting the growth of crops. This reflects the importance of biodiversity in agriculture.

White Dew

Lunar Phase: White Dew
Lunar Date: Around the 15th to 18th day of the 7th lunar month
Calendar Date: Approximately September 7th or 8th
Bird Species: Swan
Feature: The swan floats on the water like a small boat.
Design Concept: In traditional Chinese culture, White Dew heralds the coolness and harvest of autumn. The swan, dancing elegantly in this season, symbolizes grace and tranquility, like a poetic stroke on autumn waters.

The Autumnal Equinox

Lunar Phase: The Autumnal Equinox
Lunar Date: Around the 15th to 18th day of the 8th lunar month
Calendar Date: Approximately September 23rd or 24th
Bird Species: Wild Goose
Feature: The wild goose spreads its wings in the sky, with a streamlined body and strong, broad wings.
Design Concept: In China, the wild goose is imbued with a rich tapestry of cultural symbolism and is one of the most renowned and beloved bird species. Among its many associations, its behavior of migrating southward in autumn frequently links it to this season, symbolizing the sentiment of homesickness and the sorrow of parting that travelers often feel.

The Beginning of Winter

Lunar Phase: The Beginning of Winter
Lunar Date: Around the 1st to 3rd day of the 10th lunar month
Calendar Date: Approximately November 7th or 8th
Bird Species: Crow
Feature: The crow stands on a branch, upright and sturdy. Its eyes are large and bright, overlooking the world.
Design Concept: Crows, as a highly adaptable and year-round visible bird species, remain active in winter and are often featured in descriptions of the winter season in China. They are seen perched on bare tree branches or flying in the bleak sky, complementing the quiet and contemplative mood of winter days, becoming a part of the winter landscape.

Great Cold

Lunar Phase: Great Cold
Lunar Date: Around the 15th to 17th day of the 12th lunar month
Calendar Date: Approximately January 20th or 21st
Bird Species: Owl
Feature: The owl has a large, round head and eyes, with wide wings.
Design Concept: In Chinese culture, the owl, due to its nocturnal nature and ability to adapt to the cold, often symbolizes solitude and indifference. During the Great Cold, the owl's solitary activities in the quiet winter nights mirror the frigid and stern atmosphere of the season, when all is silent and still.
